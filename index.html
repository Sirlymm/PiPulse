📖Pi Network Basics
Pi Network is a cryptocurrency project founded in 2019 by Stanford graduates Nicolas Kokkalis and Chengdiao Fan. It lets users mine Pi via a mobile app without draining battery or data, using the Stellar Consensus Protocol.

Official site ↗
You tap a button once every 24 hours in the Pi app to confirm you're not a bot and continue mining. Your mining rate increases by building a security circle of trusted contacts. No battery drain, no GPU required.
A security circle is a group of 3–5 people you trust who you vouch for. It strengthens the Pi network's trust graph and increases your mining rate. Only add people you genuinely know and trust.
🔄Migration Guide
Migration is the process of moving your mined Pi from the test network to the real mainnet blockchain. Once migrated, your Pi becomes a real, tradeable asset on the Pi blockchain.
You need to: (1) Complete KYC identity verification inside the Pi app, (2) Submit a migration application, (3) Choose your lockup period for bonus mining rewards, (4) Confirm your security circle is active. Then wait for Pi Core Team approval.
A lockup period means voluntarily locking a portion of your Pi for a set time. In exchange you earn a higher mining rate bonus. Locked Pi cannot be transferred until the period ends.

🚀Pi Launchpad
Pi Launchpad is Pi Network's platform for launching new tokens and projects built on the Pi blockchain. Projects undergo KYB (Know Your Business) verification and can raise awareness and funding from the Pioneer community.
Check if the project has KYB verification. Read the whitepaper or project description carefully. Look at the team's background. Be cautious of projects promising guaranteed returns. Always do your own research (DYOR).
